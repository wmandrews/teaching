Digital Frameworks

Our focus

Data has never been as plentiful, and important, in human history. It shapes business decisions and government policies, but understanding data and visualizing it clearly and without bias is still rare.

When properly produced, data visualization and graphics can be a most-effective storytelling technique. Our goal is to understand when and why to use visuals to tell a story, and how to shape data into appropriate and powerful visual storytelling.

What we'll do in class

In each class, we'll take a look at a set of skills, tools and examples that focus on a theme.

Data

Getting from raw data and reporting to a story requires careful editing, analysis and research. How can we analyze and "interview" data to help tell better stories?

Charts

Effective visualization using charts can provide a compelling and clear story. How can we create clean, accessible charts for the web using the data we've found and analyzed?

Maps

Millions of people carry maps around in their pocket every day in the form of a phone. Geographic knowledge and understanding is greater than ever, and the tools to create maps are rich and powerful. What makes a good map, and when is a map the correct way to tell a story?

Open source

Collaboration and freedom are core values of the web. How can we use open source resources and also apply the lessons of open source to journalism?

What you'll do between sessions

Each week you will turn in an assignment related to the previous week's topic. You are also expected to introduce yourself to the next session's material by reading and completing the assigned prep work.
